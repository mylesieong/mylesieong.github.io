Devices

The app uses what you already carry and wear to show you’re real and present. For this MVP, you need a phone paired with a compatible wearable so biometrics can reach the app—a phone alone isn’t enough. Tap a tag for platform requirements.

android Android

SecurAvatars for Android runs on Android 7.0 (Nougat) and newer — so most phones from the last several years are supported. If your device can install current apps from Google Play, you’re likely good to go.

Pair your phone with a compatible wearable so heart-rate data can reach the app; see Wearable for supported bands and watches.

phone_iphone iOS

You’ll need an iPhone running iOS 18.2 or later . Update to the latest iOS your model supports if you’re unsure—then install SecurAvatars from the App Store when it’s available.

Like on Android, a paired wearable is part of the MVP: some watches need a bridge app, which we list under Wearable.

watch Wearable

Use a band or watch paired with your phone that supports Heart Rate Profile broadcast over Bluetooth Low Energy (or otherwise matches our integrations). Exact behavior varies by vendor.

Compatible devices

  • · Xiaomi Smart Band 10 (recommended)
  • ·
    Apple Watch Series 10 / Ultra 3 (with bridge app)

    Use HeartCast on iPhone and Apple Watch, allow Health access, then tap "Start" on the phone—SecurAvatars then sees live HR over Bluetooth like a standard chest strap.

  • ·
    Samsung Galaxy Watch 7 / Ultra (with bridge app)

    On the watch, install Heart for Bluetooth from Play, open it, tap start, allow body-sensor permission—it broadcasts BLE heart rate your phone (and SecurAvatars) can pick up.

  • ·
    Google Pixel Watch 3 / 4 (with bridge app)

    Prefer Heart for Bluetooth on the watch and start broadcasting—or use Connected Fitness in quick settings (heart with curved lines) to share HR with compatible phone apps.

  • · Fitbit Charge 6
  • · Garmin HRM-Pro and other Garmin products
  • · Polar H10 and other Polar products
  • · Other sport bands with Heart Rate Profile broadcast over BLE
