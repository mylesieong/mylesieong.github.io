Calmly News

Overview

Most news apps are designed to keep you addicted. I built Calmly News to do the opposite: keep you informed, and then help you get off your phone.

I call it a "cooling system" for your digital life. It's not about ignoring the world; it's about engaging with it without burning out.

How it works:

  • Just the facts: AI strips away the "drama adjectives" and emotional language. You get the actual news, not the clickbait designed to make you angry or scared.

Follow the breadcrumb: micro innovation on a proven genre

A proven market product enhanced with innovative features, delivered in 4 weeks through focused collaboration and a structured workflow.

Motivation & Problem

I identified a clear gap in the market: while many Bible reader apps exist, most suffer from poor user experience and lack features that address real user needs. New users often struggle to understand verses without contextual explanations, creating a barrier to engagement. I decided to build a better Bible reader specifically designed for this group of users—one that combines exceptional UX with AI-powered verse explanations to make Bible reading more accessible and meaningful.

Team & Collaboration

I worked with two partners in a structured, time-limited workflow:

  • My Role (Developer): Technical implementation, AI integration, and feature development. I consistently reminded the team to estimate business value and cost when evaluating features, which proved to be an excellent practice for maintaining focus.
  • Partner 1: User interface design—responsible for creating an exceptional, user-friendly interface
  • Partner 2: Product strategy and marketing—handled product requirements, market research, and go-to-market strategies

4-Week Development Process

Week 1: Building the MVP Foundation

Our first week was focused on creating a functional starting point. I developed a minimal Bible reader with core reading functionality, while the UI team worked in parallel to design an exceptional interface. Simultaneously, the product team researched solutions to address the verse explanation challenge.

Outcome: A standard Bible reader with minimal but functional UI, with design work progressing in parallel.

Week 2: Innovation & Enhancement

Week two brought significant upgrades. The UI team delivered their polished interface design, which I integrated. More importantly, we implemented our core innovation: an AI-powered solution to explain verses on demand, directly addressing the user pain point we identified.

Outcome: A beta product with an upgraded UI and AI-powered verse explanations—already covering basic features and solving a critical user problem.

Week 3: Publication & Refinement

With the basic features complete, servers deployed, and UI finalized, we began the app store publication process early in week three. Understanding that store approval involves auditing and potential back-and-forth, we didn't wait. While the app underwent review, we continued polishing details and fixing bugs to ensure the best possible user experience.

Outcome: A published app on both App Store and Play Store that is fully usable and features our core innovation—all within three weeks.

Week 4: Marketing & Monetization

The final week focused on growth and sustainability. The development team integrated third-party analytics tools to track user engagement. The marketing team created assets for advertising campaigns, and the product team worked on collecting real user reviews and feedback to improve search engine optimization.

We also developed our pricing model and integrated advertisements into the app, establishing a sustainable monetization strategy.

Outcome: A complete product with integrated marketing tools, monetization strategy, and analytics—ready for user acquisition and growth.

Key Learnings & Methodology

The time-limited workflow proved invaluable. By assigning a clear theme to each week, we maintained focus on the most important aspects:

  • Week 1: MVP foundation
  • Week 2: Innovation and core features
  • Week 3: Publication and polish
  • Week 4: Marketing and monetization

This structure forced our team to come together regularly and discuss what truly mattered, preventing scope creep and ensuring we delivered value at each stage. My role in prompting business value vs. cost evaluations helped the team make informed decisions about feature priorities.

Outcomes & Results

  • Successfully published app on both App Store and Play Store within 3 weeks
  • Delivered innovative AI-powered verse explanation feature that addresses a real user need
  • Established sustainable monetization through ad integration and pricing strategy
  • Integrated analytics and SEO optimization for long-term growth
  • Demonstrated effective partnership model with clear roles and time-bound deliverables

Build a production level social network in 4 weeks

An ongoing product building a social network for a vertical community, using the same proven 4-week methodology with flexibility to adapt to project complexities.

Motivation & Challenge

Building a social network for a vertical community presents unique technical challenges. The project requires complex backend systems to handle user interactions, content management, and blockchain integrations, while maintaining a smooth and intuitive user experience. My goal is to create a platform that serves a vertical community without overwhelming users with technical complexity.

Team & Collaboration

Similar to my Bible Project workflow, I'm working with partners who handle different aspects of the product:

  • My Role (Developer): Technical implementation, backend systems, and feature prioritization. I consistently guide the team to evaluate business value versus development cost for each feature, ensuring we focus on what matters most.
  • Partner(s): UI design, marketing, and product management—working collaboratively to deliver a cohesive product

4-Week Development Process

Week 1: Core User Experience First

Given the backend complexity inherent in social networks, we made a strategic decision: build a working UI/UX application first. This allowed us to establish the major user experience flows before diving into the backend infrastructure. This approach ensures we're building the right features from a user perspective, not just technically feasible ones.

Outcome: A functional UI prototype showcasing core user experience flows and design direction.

Week 2: Backend Integration & MVP Completion

With the user experience defined, week two focused on building out the service side—implementing the backend systems, APIs, and integrations needed to make the app fully functional. We polished features and refined the user experience based on what we learned from week one.

Outcome: A fully functional MVP product with polished features and refined user experience, ready for internal team testing.

Week 3: Public Internal Testing Preparation

Week three involved defining a committed scope—a clear set of final tasks that needed completion before launching public internal testing. The development team focused on fixing bugs and adding missing critical features, while the marketing team organized focus groups to ensure we were moving in the same direction with the same goals.

Outcome: Product launched into public internal testing phase, with feedback collection mechanisms in place.

Week 4: Publication (Upcoming)

The product is currently undergoing public internal testing, which requires time to gather meaningful feedback. Once we've collected and analyzed user feedback, we'll resume work for week four, which will focus on publishing the application on both App Store and Play Store based on the insights we've gained.

Current Status: Public internal testing in progress—collecting user feedback to inform final adjustments before publication.

Flexible Methodology & Key Principles

While I use the same 4-week template, it's intentionally flexible. Each week's theme doesn't have to be exactly the same across projects because different products have different needs. However, the structure provides valuable benefits:

  • Clear Weekly Themes: Each week has a focused goal, keeping the team aligned
  • Reasonable Timeline: Four weeks is achievable with AI assistance and limited resources, but still creates urgency that drives focused decision-making
  • Forced Prioritization: The time constraint ensures the team comes together regularly to discuss what truly matters, preventing feature bloat
  • Business Value Focus: My consistent reminders to estimate business value and development cost helps the team make data-driven decisions about feature priorities

Current Outcomes & Next Steps

  • Completed functional MVP with polished user experience and backend integration
  • Successfully launched public internal testing phase
  • Gathered focus group feedback to validate direction
  • Organized team around clear scope and committed deliverables
  • Next: Analyze feedback, make final adjustments, and publish to App Store and Play Store
